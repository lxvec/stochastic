Foundational Concepts

A partition of the sample space Ω divides it into disjoint atoms. The σ-algebra generated by a partition contains all finite unions of atoms, giving 2^n sets where n is the number of atoms.

A filtration {F_t} is a sequence of nested σ-algebras: F₀ ⊆ F₁ ⊆ F₂ ⊆ ... It models the evolution of information over time.

E[X | F] is the best prediction of X given the information in F. It is constant on atoms of F, representing the average value of X on each atom.

A martingale satisfies E[S_{t+1} | F_t] = S_t, meaning it has no drift. Under the risk-neutral measure, discounted stock prices are martingales, enabling option pricing.

A discrete-time model where at each step, the stock multiplies by u (up) or d (down). Options are priced by backward induction using risk-neutral probabilities.
