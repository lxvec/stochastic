Foundational Concepts

A partition of the sample space Œ© divides it into disjoint atoms. The œÉ-algebra generated by a partition contains all finite unions of atoms, giving 2^n sets where n is the number of atoms.

A filtration {F_t} is a sequence of nested œÉ-algebras: F‚ÇÄ ‚äÜ F‚ÇÅ ‚äÜ F‚ÇÇ ‚äÜ ... It models the evolution of information over time.

E[X | F] is the best prediction of X given the information in F. It is constant on atoms of F, representing the average value of X on each atom.

A martingale satisfies E[S_{t+1} | F_t] = S_t, meaning it has no drift. Under the risk-neutral measure, discounted stock prices are martingales, enabling option pricing.
