Filtrations & Information Evolution

A filtration is a nested sequence of σ-algebras: F₀ ⊆ F₁ ⊆ F₂ ⊆ ... representing information revealed over time. Each step reveals more about the sample space structure.

Mathematical Definition

Filtration: A sequence {Ft}t≥0 of σ-algebras such that:

  • F₀ ⊆ F₁ ⊆ F₂ ⊆ ... ⊆ F (non-decreasing)
  • Each Ft represents the information available at time t
  • If an event was observed at time s ≤ t, it remains observable at time t

Example: At each step, we observe an outcome of a random variable Xt, and the new information is σ(Ft ∪ {Xt}), the σ-algebra generated by both the previous information and the new observation.
